Chasing Dreams: Start-Ups in the Holy City


The intention of this website is to highlight and draw attention to exceptional Charleston area small businesses and start-ups.


Water Dog Paddle Co. is the stand-up paddleboard specialist for Kiawah, Seabrook & Johns Islands. We offer guided dolphin paddles, custom private paddles, group paddles, rentals & sales. Our goal is to provide our guests with a positive paddleboarding experience, while enjoying the beauty of the salt marshes, waterways & wildlife in the sea islands of Charleston. Whether first-timer or advanced paddler,Water Dog is here to meet your needs
Fatty’s Beer Works aims to create and cultivate the ultimate in original recipes throughout the course of human history to extol to the world with the greatness of what craft beer can be. I got to talk to the man behind the beer, David McLain recently and am very thrilled to announce that Fatty’s will be coming to you very shortly. Fatty’s first beer release, “First Shot” IPA, traveled beer festivals across SC leaving many kicked kegs in its wake. The crowds loved First Shot IPA for the rich orange pour, and distinct assertive bitterness that really helps round out the flavor. Along with hop flavors of resiny-pine, it’s no wonder “First Shot” IPA has been a revelation at multiple beer festivals and headlining tasting events.
The ACE Basin Express looks forward to offering you the best solution for travel between Charleston and Savannah. Founded in 2015, we seek to fill a growing demand among travelers and residents to experience Charleston and Savannah without the burden of a car. Forget about the one-way streets, parking fees, and designated drivers. These cities are best explored on foot, and now there is a simple, seamless, locally-owned and operated service that will shuttle you between the two.
Congress is the work of couple Mark and Adrienne Ciaburri, who moved from Austin to Charleston several years ago. The duo met and got engaged in Austin, however, Adrienne is a Mount Pleasant native, whose first job was waiting tables at The Wreck. The team completely gutted the former La Ha space to make way for the "upscale/casual New American restaurant." The menu is influenced by Mark's background in Italian and interior Mexican cuisine. A look at the restaurant's Facebook page reveals potential menu items like skirt steak with chimichurri, chili relleno stuffed with short rib and havarti, and snapper ceviche with pomegranate seeds, cucumber, and jicama. .
